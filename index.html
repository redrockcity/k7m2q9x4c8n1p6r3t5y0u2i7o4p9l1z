Academic discipleship

A school built around partnership, purpose, and preparation

Crestmont is a non-denominational K-12 Christian school dedicated to high-quality education, servant leadership, and academic preparation. Our University-Model structure is designed to support family partnership while helping students grow in independence, discipline, and love of learning.

  • University-Model education. Our schedule helps cultivate time management and independent study skills that prepare students for college and life.
  • Christian formation across the school day. We design every lesson and activity to help raise servant leaders and warriors for Christ.
  • Family partnership from the start. Our admissions process is built around shared fit, with research, Family Information Meetings, interviews, and student assessment.
Why families choose Crestmont

Here is a quick overview of who we are and what makes this season distinctive.

Historic downtown campus story

A historic schoolhouse becomes a school again

For the 2026-27 school year, we are moving to 402 E. Blanco in downtown Boerne. Built in 1910, this building once served as Boerne's first high school and later as city hall. Now it is returning to its original educational purpose.

Heart of Boerne campus Our new campus brings Crestmont into the heart of Boerne.
What this move means for families: Our new campus gives us a more visible home in Boerne, a building with deep local history, and space we are preparing for the 2026-27 school year.

Founding Families

Founding Families of the Heart of Boerne Campus

Families who join us for the 2026-27 school year will be recognized as Founding Families of the Heart of Boerne campus, honoring their trust as we begin this new chapter downtown.

Recognizing the first downtown cohort
  • Recognition in a future roster or display, if families opt in.
  • Invitation to a pre-opening welcome gathering or prayer evening.
  • A signed note from school leadership.
  • Inclusion in a first-year commemorative piece, if produced.
  • An opening-season group photo tied to the new campus chapter.
What that includes

A simple way to mark a meaningful year

Founding Families is meant to be simple and meaningful: a way to honor this first downtown cohort without turning enrollment into a membership program.

  • Recognition with gratitude. Acknowledgment may include a roster or display, a welcome event, and a note from school leadership.
  • Connection to the new campus. The focus is on joining Crestmont at a defining moment in our school's story.
  • No special tier or discount. The emphasis is recognition, not exclusivity.
